Freesia





Introduction

Freesia are perennial flowers in the iris family. They resemble their cousins, having long narrow stems like an iris and colorful cup-shaped flowers like a crocus. Freesia flowers can be almost any color, and while most varieties are solid colors, there are a few two-toned varieties. Like other plants in their family, freesias can grow from corms or from seeds, but they typically grow from bulbs, as its quicker.

About

Freesia, (genus Freesia), genus of about 20 species perennial plants of the iris family native to southern Africa. The plants are grown indoors in pots or in gardens in mild climates, and the cut flowers are important in the floral industry.
